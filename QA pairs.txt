Q1. What are the most common types of cancer worldwide?
The most common cancers are breast, lung, colon and rectum, and prostate cancers.

Q2. What major factors are responsible for about one-third of all cancer deaths?
Tobacco use, high body mass index, alcohol consumption, low fruit and vegetable intake, and lack of physical activity. Air pollution is also an important risk factor for lung cancer.

Q3. How can the risk of cancer be reduced according to the text?
By not using tobacco, maintaining a healthy body weight, eating fruits and vegetables, exercising regularly, limiting alcohol, getting vaccinated against HPV and hepatitis B, avoiding UV exposure, and reducing air pollution exposure.

Q4. What is the difference between early diagnosis and screening in cancer detection?
Early diagnosis deals with identifying and evaluating symptoms as soon as possible, while screening checks people without symptoms to detect early or pre-cancerous changes.

Q5. What are the main forms of cancer treatment mentioned?
Surgery, radiotherapy, and systemic therapy, which includes chemotherapy, hormonal therapy, and targeted biological treatments.

Q6. Why is palliative care important for cancer patients?
It helps relieve pain and suffering, improves quality of life, and supports patients physically, emotionally, and spiritually, especially in advanced stages.
